Dog Photography FAQ


Everything you might want to know before your dog’s photography session.

Choosing a photographer for your dog is a big decision, and it’s natural to have questions. Here are some of the things clients often ask before their session.

Before the Session

I am concerned that my dog will be nervous with new people.

Many dogs are unsure around new people at first. Sessions are relaxed and unhurried, giving your dog time to explore the environment and feel comfortable before we start. Patience and positive reinforcement help bring out their natural personality.

My dog won’t sit still for photos.

That’s completely normal. Dog photography focuses on capturing natural moments, movement, and personality rather than forcing perfect poses.

How do I know if the photographer is good?

A strong portfolio should show consistency across different dogs, lighting conditions, and locations. Experience working with animals is just as important as technical photography skill.

My dog cannot be off lead.

That’s absolutely fine. Many action shots are taken with dogs safely on lead and edited afterwards so the lead is no longer visible.

What happens if the weather is bad?

If the weather isn’t suitable we simply reschedule. Outdoor sessions work best when both you and your dog are comfortable.

During the Session

I don’t think my dog would relax in a studio.

Sessions take place outdoors in natural locations such as woodland areas where dogs feel far more relaxed and able to be themselves.

Can I choose the style of photos?

Absolutely. Sessions can focus on action, portraits, or a mixture of both depending on the personality of your dog and what you would like to capture.

Can I be in the photos with my dog?

Of course. Many people love having a few images that capture the bond between them and their dog.

What do I need to bring with me?

Not much at all.

A lead, a few of your dog’s favourite treats, and maybe a toy they love are perfect.

If your dog has a favourite blanket or bandana you’d like in the photos, feel free to bring that too. Otherwise, just bring your dog as they are — curiosity, muddy paws and all.

As sessions take place outdoors, it’s also best to wear comfortable clothing and footwear you don’t mind getting a little muddy, depending on the weather.

I’ll guide you through everything during the session.

After the Session

How will I view the images?

After your session you will receive access to a private online gallery where you can view the photographs and select your favourites.

Can I order prints?

Yes. High-quality professional prints are available so you can enjoy your dog’s portraits on the wall rather than just on a screen.

Special Situations

Do you photograph older dogs?

Absolutely. Senior dogs often make some of the most meaningful photographs. Sessions can be adapted so they remain relaxed and comfortable.

What if my dog has mobility or medical needs?

Sessions can be tailored to your dog’s needs. The goal is always to create a calm, safe environment where your dog can simply be themselves.



If you have any other questions about dog photography sessions, you are always welcome to get in touch.
