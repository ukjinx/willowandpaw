Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

Willow & Paw Photography is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what information is collected, why it is collected, and how it is handled in line with UK data protection law.

1. Who We Are

Willow & Paw Photography is a sole trader photography business based in South Wales, United Kingdom.

For any privacy-related enquiries, please contact:
willowandpawphoto@gmail.com

2. What Information Is Collected

When you enquire or book a session, the following information may be collected:

  • Your name
  • Email address
  • Phone number (if provided)
  • Session location details
  • Information about your dog relevant to the session

Website analytics data may also be collected anonymously to help improve the site.

3. How Your Information Is Used

Your information is used only to:

  • Respond to enquiries
  • Manage and deliver photography sessions
  • Process payments
  • Deliver images
  • Comply with legal and accounting obligations

Your information is never sold or shared for marketing purposes.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, personal data is processed on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract — to provide photography services you have requested
  • Legal obligation — for tax and accounting requirements
  • Legitimate interest — to operate and improve the business

5. How Your Data Is Stored

Personal data is stored securely using password-protected systems. Images are backed up securely.

Client information is retained only for as long as necessary for business, legal, and accounting purposes (typically up to 6 years where required by law).

6. Image Usage

Photographs may be used for portfolio, website, or social media purposes unless you request otherwise in writing.

You may opt out of promotional use at any time.

7. Your Rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal data
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data (where legally permitted)
  • Object to certain types of processing

Requests can be made via email.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK’s data protection authority, the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO), if you believe your data has been handled improperly.

8. Cookies

This website uses cookies to ensure it functions properly and to understand how visitors use the site.

Analytics cookies (such as Google Analytics) help track anonymous usage data including pages visited, time spent on the site, and general location data. This helps improve the website experience.

These cookies are only set after you provide consent via the cookie banner.

You can withdraw or change your cookie preferences at any time by clearing cookies in your browser settings.

9. Third-Party Services

This website uses trusted third-party services to operate effectively. These may include:

  • Google Analytics (website usage tracking)
  • Email providers (for responding to enquiries)
  • Hosting providers (to deliver the website)

These providers may process data on our behalf in accordance with their own privacy policies and applicable data protection laws.

10. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated occasionally to reflect changes in law or business practices.

Your trust matters. Your information is handled with the same care and respect as your photographs.

I use cookies to understand how visitors interact with the site and improve your experience.
